**1. Executive Summary**

Delhi Green Guardians (DGG) respectfully requests ₹2,075,000 from the Department of Environmental Quality's Regional Waterway Restoration Fund to implement the "Najafgarh Drain Ecological Restoration and Community Engagement Project." This vital initiative targets a critically polluted 1.5-kilometer stretch of the Najafgarh Drain in Delhi, aiming to significantly reduce solid waste accumulation, improve water flow, and foster enduring community stewardship. The project will achieve an estimated removal of **80-100 metric tons of solid waste** and engage **over 300 unique local volunteers** through targeted clean-up drives and comprehensive awareness campaigns. By restoring this crucial urban waterway, we aim to enhance local ecological health, mitigate public health risks, and establish a sustainable model for community-led environmental action in Delhi, directly contributing to the broader ecological health of the Yamuna River basin.

**2. Ecological Impact Assessment**

The Najafgarh Drain, historically a natural stormwater channel and a significant tributary to the Yamuna River, has regrettably transformed into a primary carrier of untreated sewage, industrial effluent, and indiscriminate solid waste. The targeted 1.5-kilometer section suffers from extreme plastic pollution, non-biodegradable debris, and heavy siltation, leading to severe ecological degradation and public health concerns:

*   **Habitat Destruction:** The vast accumulation of waste smothers aquatic life, drastically reduces dissolved oxygen levels, and prevents the re-establishment of native flora and fauna essential for a healthy aquatic ecosystem.
*   **Water Quality Deterioration:** Leachates from decomposing organic matter and plastics introduce harmful chemicals, raising Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD) and Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) levels, making the water toxic and contributing to groundwater contamination.
*   **Increased Flood Risk:** Obstruction by solid waste and silt reduces the drain's carrying capacity, exacerbating localized flooding in adjacent densely populated areas during monsoon seasons.
*   **Public Health Hazards:** Stagnant, polluted water serves as a prolific breeding ground for disease vectors such as mosquitoes, contributing to a high incidence of water-borne diseases like dengue, malaria, and cholera in surrounding informal settlements.
*   **Aesthetic Degradation:** The visual blight and foul odour significantly reduce the quality of life for residents living near the drain.

Our project will deliver tangible ecological benefits through:

*   **Direct Waste Removal:** Manual and mechanical removal of all solid waste (plastics, cloth, construction debris, organic waste) from the drain banks and waterbody. This is estimated to remove **80-100 metric tons of solid waste**.
*   **Improved Water Flow:** Clearing obstructions and targeted minor desilting will improve the drain's hydraulic efficiency, reducing stagnation and localized flooding.
*   **Pollution Load Reduction:** By intercepting waste before it enters the Yamuna River, the project will contribute to a reduced pollution burden on the larger river ecosystem.
*   **Biodiversity Recovery (Initial Phase):** The removal of physical pollutants and initial improvement in water quality will create conditions conducive to the return of tolerant aquatic insects, some fish species, and riparian vegetation, fostering a nascent recovery of the local ecosystem.
*   **Enhanced Public Health:** Reducing breeding grounds for disease vectors directly improves public health outcomes for vulnerable communities.

**3. Volunteer Mobilization Plan**

The success and sustainability of the "Najafgarh Drain Ecological Restoration and Community Engagement Project" are fundamentally linked to robust community participation and sustained volunteer engagement. Delhi Green Guardians has a strong track record of mobilizing diverse community groups for environmental initiatives across Delhi. Our comprehensive plan includes:

*   **Community Outreach and Awareness Campaigns:**
    *   **Local Consultations:** Conduct initial meetings with Resident Welfare Associations (RWAs), community leaders, local youth groups, and market associations along the targeted stretch to introduce the project, explain its benefits, and solicit initial support.
    *   **Educational Workshops:** Organize interactive workshops in local schools, colleges, and community centers focusing on waste segregation, the ecological importance of urban waterways, and the direct link between drain cleanliness and public health.
    *   **Information Dissemination:** Develop and distribute multi-lingual (Hindi and English) flyers, posters, and leverage local social media platforms to raise awareness and invite active participation.
*   **Volunteer Recruitment and Training:**
    *   **Targeted Recruitment Drives:** Set up information kiosks at local festivals, markets, and university campuses to recruit volunteers from diverse backgrounds, including students, senior citizens, and local residents.
    *   **Mandatory Safety Briefings:** All volunteers will undergo a comprehensive safety induction covering safe waste handling protocols, proper usage of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), first aid procedures, and on-site emergency contact information.
    *   **Role-Specific Training:** Provide brief, practical training for various tasks such as litter picking, waste segregation (into plastics, organic, inert), data collection, and community liaison roles.
*   **Structured Clean-up Drives:**
    *   **Phased Implementation:** Organize 6-8 large-scale clean-up drives over the project duration, each targeting a specific section of the 1.5 km stretch, ensuring thorough coverage. Each drive will aim to engage 30-50 volunteers.
    *   **Logistical Support:** Provide all necessary equipment, including gloves, masks, safety vests, boots, rakes, shovels, litter pickers, and heavy-duty waste bags. Fresh drinking water, light refreshments, and a fully equipped first-aid station will be available at all drive locations.
    *   **Supervision:** Experienced Field Supervisors will be present at all times to guide volunteers, ensure safety, and manage waste collection logistics.
*   **Sustained Engagement and Ownership:**
    *   **"Apna Drain, Apni Zimmedari" (Our Drain, Our Responsibility) Program:** Encourage local residents and community groups to "adopt-a-spot" for ongoing monitoring and minor maintenance post-major clean-up drives.
    *   **Citizen Science Initiatives:** Engage volunteers in simple water quality monitoring (using basic kits) and waste auditing to foster long-term interest and data collection.
    *   **Recognition and Appreciation:** Acknowledge and appreciate volunteer efforts through certificates of participation, small tokens of gratitude, and public recognition via local media and DGG's communication channels.

**Estimated Number of Volunteers Engaged:** We project to engage a minimum of **300 unique volunteers** throughout the project duration, with many participating in multiple drives, leading to over 1,500 cumulative volunteer hours. This deep level of engagement will foster a sense of collective ownership and responsibility for the Najafgarh Drain's long-term health.
