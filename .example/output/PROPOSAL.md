# GRANT PROPOSAL: National River Conservation and Cleanup Grant

**Organization:** CleanWaters NGO
**Proposed Budget:** ₹1,800,000 INR
**Location:** Varanasi
**Duration:** 6 months

---

## Executive Summary

**Executive Summary: CleanWaters NGO - Ganga River Ghats Cleanup Project, Varanasi**     

CleanWaters NGO proudly presents a comprehensive proposal for the National River Conservation and Cleanup Grant, issued by the Ministry of Jal Shakti, seeking ₹1,800,000 INR. This six-month initiative is strategically designed to rejuvenate the iconic Ganga River ghats in Varanasi, a site of immense cultural and ecological significance. Our project aims to directly address the critical issue of plastic pollution by meticulously removing an estimated 50 tonnes of plastic waste from the river and its banks.

Beyond immediate cleanup, the project prioritizes community engagement, intending to mobilize and train 100 local volunteers. This volunteer involvement will foster a sustainable model for river conservation and instill a sense of collective responsibility within the community. The requested budget will be allocated judiciously across essential operational categories: ₹600,000 INR for cleanup nets, ₹600,000 INR for bio-remediation agents, and ₹600,000 INR for safety gear, ensuring the efficient and safe execution of all cleanup activities. CleanWaters NGO is committed to delivering measurable environmental improvements and empowering local communities in Varanasi for a cleaner, healthier Ganga River.

## Community Mobilization Plan

## Community Mobilization Plan

The success of the CleanWaters NGO's Ganga River Ghats Cleanup Project is intrinsically linked to robust community engagement and mobilization. This six-month initiative in Varanasi will implement a multi-faceted approach to attract and retain a dedicated volunteer base, with a specific target of engaging 100 local volunteers throughout the project duration.

Our strategy will encompass:

*   **Local Outreach Programs:** CleanWaters NGO will conduct targeted outreach in key communities along the Ganga River ghats in Varanasi. This will involve informational sessions at local community centers, schools, and religious institutions to raise awareness about the critical need for river conservation and the tangible benefits of local participation.
*   **Volunteer Recruitment Drives:** Regular volunteer recruitment drives will be organized at prominent public spaces, leveraging local leaders and existing community networks to amplify our message. We will highlight the direct impact volunteers will have on improving local environmental health and cultural heritage.
*   **Capacity Building and Training:** Newly recruited volunteers, alongside our existing team of 10 dedicated individuals, will receive comprehensive training. This will cover safe waste collection practices, segregation techniques, and the importance of bio-remediation efforts. Training will ensure volunteers are well-equipped and confident in their roles.
*   **Recognition and Retention:** To foster a sustainable volunteer ecosystem, CleanWaters NGO will implement a recognition program acknowledging the invaluable contributions of our community members. This will include public recognition events, certificates of appreciation, and opportunities for skill development, ensuring long-term engagement beyond the project's initial six months.
*   **Collaboration with Local Stakeholders:** We will actively collaborate with local municipal bodies, educational institutions, and other non-governmental organizations in Varanasi to co-promote volunteer opportunities and integrate cleanup activities into broader community development initiatives. This collaborative approach will strengthen the project's roots within the local social fabric.

By implementing these strategies, CleanWaters NGO is confident in its ability to not only meet but potentially exceed the goal of engaging 100 local volunteers, transforming them into stewards of the Ganga River and ensuring the long-term sustainability of cleanliness efforts in Varanasi.
